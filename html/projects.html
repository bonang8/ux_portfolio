Engage

Improving Higher Education Using IoT Devices One Lecture at a Time

Last quarter, I had the pleasure of teaching a computer science course at Chapman University. Teaching brought valuable insights into the activities that keep students engaged, improving their overall learning retention. Researching the literature on behavioral changes during lectures as well devices to aid student learning and monitor student attention span, and reviewing studies on how teachers evaluate the effectiveness of their lectures helped me better understand classroom dynamics to support student learning. It led me to the following research question: How might we assist professional educators to improve their lectures by monitoring students attention using Internet-of-Things (IoT) devices? Improving lectures by time-stamping periods of high and low student attention along with student feedback formed the basis for my Engage project

 

Netflix

Improving Netflix's Competitiveness and User Experience

This project is focused on improving competitiveness through creating a better user experience. This design study involves conducting expert evaluations, user evaluations, and ultimately recommendations to improve the the user experience.

Expert evaluations involved heuristic evaluation using Jakob Nielsen's 10 heuristics to discover interactive hindrances with the user interface and competitive analysis to have a better understanding of how Netflix can excel and differentiate in the competitive landscape.

Seeker

Improving Information Literacy and Library Accessibility for Blind and Visually Impaired Students

This project is focused on helping blind and visually impaired students with library accessibility and improving information literacy. Seeker is an application that supports blind and visually impaired students in achieving independence in their academic pursuits and research, including auditory assistance, beepers to direct movement, voice instructions, and vibrations as well as quad buttons Created a persuasive deck, system flow, and high fidelity prototype using Figma that follows blind and visually impaired design guidelines and adopted interactive practices.
