Engage

Improving Higher Education Using IoT Devices One Lecture at a Time

As a lecturer at Chapman University, I gained insights into effective student engagement and learning retention. My research explored behavioral changes during lectures, devices for student learning, and teacher evaluation methods. This led to a research question: How can we enhance lectures by monitoring student attention with IoT devices? My Engage project focuses on time-stamping attention levels and incorporating student feedback. In my role, I created a User Empathy Map, conducted Literature Reviews, designed Initial Sketches, developed a System Flow Chart, crafted a Smart Environment Visualization, and produced a Low-Fidelity Prototype for testing and refinement. AR University

Improving Education One Tap at a Time

The prototype leverages Large Language Models (LLMs), the Ru chatbot, and Reality Interaction Modules (RIM) to empower independent learning. My contributions include crafting a conceptual model, sketches, literature review and the mid-fidelity prototype. The Ru chatbot guides students without disclosing solutions, while RIM facilitates object identification and data collection. Augmented Reality (AR) tools have been seamlessly integrated to heighten engagement, offering visual support for student learning. This amalgamation of technologies is exemplified in the AR University Presentation, showcasing the innovative potential of this multifaceted learning approach. AR University Presentation

 

Netflix

Improving Netflix's Competitiveness and User Experience

Engaged in user interviews, expert evaluations, and prototyping, this project is dedicated to enhancing competitiveness through an improved user experience. The design study integrates Jakob Nielsen's heuristics and competitive analysis for expert evaluations, while user evaluations involve card sorting and both quantitative and qualitative usability assessments. Collaboratively with the team, my involvement in expert evaluations extended to refining search features and the footer bar. The insights gathered from user interviews and evaluations informed the prototyping phase, leading to a comprehensive executive summary for actionable improvements.

 

Seeker

Improving Information Literacy and Library Accessibility for Blind and Visually Impaired Students

This project is focused on helping blind and visually impaired students with library accessibility and improving information literacy. Seeker is an application that supports blind and visually impaired students in achieving independence in their academic pursuits and research, including auditory assistance, beepers to direct movement, voice instructions, and vibrations as well as quad buttons Created a persuasive deck, system flow, and high fidelity prototype using Figma that follows blind and visually impaired design guidelines and adopted interactive practices.

Improving Accessibility Options on Genshin Impact iOS

This report summarizes a study investigating Genshin Impact's accessibility features and their effectiveness as assessed by a broad range of players. The study employed surveys, interviews and usability testing. The usability testing was conducted on a single platform (mobile devices running the iOS operating system) and used a well-defined subset of the game called Commission Quests. The report provides both qualitative and quantitative analyses of the collected data.The report also examines potential accessibility enhancements such as, color blind mode, improvements to the user interface, and text scalability GitHub
